Atonement

Be True to God and His Work || Elder Quentin L. Cook

October 2022 general conference

Christ would take away sin. Without the Savior’s Atonement, the eternal principle of justice would require punishment. Because of the Savior’s Atonement, mercy can prevail for those who have repented, and it can allow them to return to the presence of God. We would do well to ponder this wonderful doctrine.

Be True to God and His Work || Elder Quentin L. Cook

October 2022 general conference

None can return to God by his or her own good works alone; we all need the benefit of the Savior’s sacrifice. All have sinned, and it is only through the Atonement of Jesus Christ that we can obtain mercy and live with God.

Happy and Forever || Gerrit W. Gong

October 2022 general conference

Central in God’s plan of redemption and happiness, Jesus Christ, through His Atonement, promises to unite our spirit and body, “never again to be divided, that [we] might receive a fulness of joy.

Happy and Forever || Gerrit W. Gong

October 2022 general conference

Atonement—at-one-ment in Christ—comes as we exercise faith and bring forth fruits unto repentance. As in mortality, so in immortality. Temple ordinances do not of themselves change us or those in the spirit world. But these divine ordinances enable sanctifying covenants with the Lord, which can bring harmony with Him and each other.

Lifted Up upon the Cross || By Elder Jeffrey R. Holland

October 2022 general conference

As we take up our crosses and follow Him, it would be tragic indeed if the weight of our challenges did not make us more empathetic for and more attentive to the burdens being carried by others. It is one of the most powerful paradoxes of the Crucifixion that the arms of the Savior were stretched wide open and then nailed there, unwittingly but accurately portraying that every man, woman, and child in the entire human family is not only welcome but invited into His redeeming, exalting embrace

Lifted Up upon the Cross || By Elder Jeffrey R. Holland

October 2022 general conference

As the glorious Resurrection followed the agonizing Crucifixion, so blessings of every kind are poured out on those who are willing, as the Book of Mormon prophet Jacob says, to “believe in Christ, and view his death, and suffer his cross.” Sometimes these blessings come soon and sometimes they come later, but the marvelous conclusion to our personal via dolorosa is the promise from the Master Himself that they do and will come.

Be Perfected in Him || Elder Paul V. Johnson

October 2022 general conference

No matter how capable, educated, brilliant, or strong we are, we cannot cleanse ourselves from our sins, change our bodies to an immortal state, or exalt ourselves. It is only possible through the Savior Jesus Christ and His infinite Atonement

Be Perfected in Him || Elder Paul V. Johnson

October 2022 general conference

We are “made perfect through Jesus the mediator of the new covenant, who wrought out this perfect atonement through the shedding of his own blood.” Our perfection is only possible through God’s grace

He Is Risen with Healing in His Wings || Elder Patrick Kearon

April 2022 general conference

You can survive. You have in fact already been rescued; you have already been saved—by the One who has suffered the very torment you are suffering and endured the very agony you are enduring. Jesus has overcome the abuses of this world2 to give you power to not only survive but one day, through Him, to overcome and even conquer—to completely rise above the pain, the misery, the anguish, and see them replaced by peace.

He Is Risen with Healing in His Wings || Elder Patrick Kearon

April 2022 general conference

Though it may seem impossible, feel impossible, healing can come through the miracle of the redemptive might of the Atonement of Jesus Christ, who is risen “with healing in his wings.” Our merciful Savior, victorious over darkness and depravity, has power to right all wrongs, a life-giving truth for those wronged by others.

Position Statement

Jesus Christ is my savior and my redeemer. It is a painful thing not to have your own children when you see others have children so easily. My heart bleeds and has shed many tears. My comfort comes from the knowledge that I am known and loved. The pain, I feel is only a small portion of what I could have felt if Christ had not suffered and carried my burden. The atonement covers more than my sins. My heartache, and my sorrows, help me forgive even those that will never ask for forgiveness. It is through Christ’s atonement that we are healed.
